Rishav

At approximately 12 years of age, I picked up a new language to study. My medium of study being the the six string. A subject I chose to learn, a subject that can never be altered by our faulty education system. I took up teaching guitar and music theory to show people how music has more than its superficial character, and open the gates to the world of endless creativity. Teaching at QC, I learnt truly what I believed for ages- teaching music to me is beyond the materialistic returns, it's about seeing the joy my students eyes having learnt something cool, while I had managed to teach them something that they believed to be difficult.

Abhijit

The first time I heard the violin was when I heard "the devil's trill" by Giuseppe Tartini at age 5. That was when I decided that I wanted to learn to play the violin. At age 8 I began to learn to play Carnatic classical violin and 10 years on, I still actively play. Seeing as the instrument has been in existence for about 500 years, I feel a powerful sense of history every time I play and I'm grateful for the opportunity to carry this legacy forward in my own little way through teaching and teaching at QC has been an enjoyable and fulfilling experience for me.
